HYPOTHESIS
  Pinhole-assuming foundation depth models (UniDepth) degrade under strong lens
  distortion; LiDAR few-shot self-calibration with an EXPLICIT distortion model
  (Brown-Conrady k1,k2) stays robust and overtakes them.

METHOD (controlled, known-GT) — src/calib_depth/synth_distort.py
  Inject known radial distortion k1 into a pinhole frame: inverse-warp the image
  (remap) AND forward-distort the LiDAR uv consistently, so GT intrinsics become
  (fx,fy,cx,cy,k1). Distortion strength = controlled variable. Eval on held-out
  frames, <80 m, native res, via the unified adapters (--distort flag).
  Justified heuristic: widen the learnable radial bound (0.05 -> 0.6/0.8) because
  A4 (near-pinhole) is violated BY CONSTRUCTION; the bound must exceed true |k|.
  Validated: few-shot recovers fx to <5% and depth simultaneously.

FINDINGS
  - UniDepth is robust to MILD distortion (|k1|<=0.2) but degrades monotonically
    and COLLAPSES at strong/fisheye-strength distortion (KITTI 0.091->0.284;
    nuScenes catastrophic 0.105->0.776, d1 0.083).
  - OURS stays ~flat across the whole range (explicit distortion + few-shot LiDAR
    absorbs it): KITTI AbsRel 0.09-0.12, d1 0.83-0.95.
  - CROSSOVER ~k1=-0.3 (KITTI, where our base depth is strong); on nuScenes our
    base depth is weaker so crossover is later (between -0.4 and -0.6) but by -0.6
    OURS wins by ~3x AbsRel / ~7x d1.
  - Calibration recovered throughout: fx err <5% even at k1=-0.6.

THE PUBLISHABLE CLAIM (honest, defensible)
  "Monocular metric-depth foundation models assume near-pinhole optics and fail
  under strong lens distortion. A LiDAR-anchored few-shot self-calibration with an
  explicit distortion model recovers metric depth where they fail, overtaking SOTA
  (UniDepth) by up to 2.5x AbsRel / 7x d1 at fisheye-strength distortion, from 20
  target frames." This is a gap UniDepth/Metric3D do NOT cover.

REPLICATION ACROSS A SECOND DISTORTION FAMILY — Kannala-Brandt fisheye
  (src/calib_depth/synth_fisheye.py; KITTI-360 real fisheye is LOGIN-GATED, so this
  is the de-gated equivalent: genuine KB *angular* optics on KITTI-raw, ~+-40deg FOV.)
  Strength-matching was essential: KB k1 and Brown-Conrady k1 are NOT comparable
  (KB k1=0.9 pulls edges ~18%, BC k1=-0.6 ~44%). Unmatched KB (k1<=0.9) showed
  UniDepth "robust" -> ARTIFACT. Matched-strong KB reproduces the failure:
    KITTI image_02, KB fisheye (AbsRel / d1):
    k1     UniDepth        OURS (few-shot, BC-approx, bound 6)
    0.0    0.076 / 0.970   -
    0.9    0.057 / 0.979   -
    1.5    0.073 / 0.965   -
    2.5    0.112 / 0.838   0.126 / 0.831   (tied)
    4.0    0.158 / 0.688   0.119 / 0.832   (OURS wins)
  => The robustness finding GENERALIZES across distortion families: UniDepth
     degrades under strong distortion of EITHER model; ours stays flat (~0.12) and
     overtakes at strong distortion. Our Brown-Conrady head only APPROXIMATES KB
     (atan(r)~r within FOV) so DEPTH recovers but CALIBRATION is rough (fx err
     7-52%); a native KB/MEI head would tighten calibration (future work).
