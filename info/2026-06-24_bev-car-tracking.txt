================================================================================
2026-06-24  BEV car localization + tracking from a monocular depth model
================================================================================

GOAL (user)
  Recreate a Bird's-Eye-View visualization from the best depth model (model-agnostic;
  we use UniDepth-V2-L), tracking the position of CARS only, with metrics.

METHOD  (src/calib_depth/bev_tracker.py; runs in baselines venv: nuscenes-devkit + unidepth)
  1. UniDepth-V2-L -> metric depth (camera-z) on nuScenes CAM_FRONT.
  2. GT cars from nuScenes 3-D boxes (vehicle.car/truck/bus/... ; gives both the car
     image footprint AND the GT BEV position for scoring).
  3. Per car: project box, take MEDIAN predicted depth over its 2-D footprint, back-
     project the box-centre pixel -> camera-frame point -> BEV (lateral x, forward z).
  4. Metric: BEV position error vs GT box centre (+ longitudinal/lateral, per-range).
  5. Viz: camera image w/ car boxes (pred/GT range) + top-down BEV (ego origin, FOV
     wedge, range rings, GT dots vs predicted x).
  6. Tracking (--track): walk a scene's frames, associate cars by nuScenes instance
     token, transform points cam->ego->global, draw per-car GT vs predicted BEV
     trajectories + the ego path.

RESULTS  (UniDepth-V2-L, nuScenes v1.0-mini CAM_FRONT)
  80-sample run, 744 cars:
    BEV position error: mean 6.15 m | median 2.76 m
    longitudinal (z) 5.86 m  |  lateral (x) 1.55 m
    by range: 0-20 m 2.03 | 20-40 m 3.84 | 40-80 m 9.18
  Single-scene (scene-0061, 39 frames): 234 cars, 17 tracked (>=3 frames);
    BEV err mean 7.93 / med 3.71; 0-20 2.32 | 20-40 5.32 | 40-80 12.24.

FINDINGS
  - Lateral (x) localization is good (~1.5-2 m); the error is dominated by the
    LONGITUDINAL (depth/range) axis (~6-7 m mean) and grows strongly with range
    (2 m at <20 m -> ~9-12 m at 40-80 m) -- the expected monocular failure mode.
  - In the global-frame trajectory plot, predicted tracks run PARALLEL to GT tracks
    but are offset along the viewing ray by the range error (small near the ego path,
    larger for distant cars). The tracking/association itself is exact (GT instance
    tokens), so the plot isolates depth error, not data association.

UPDATE 2 — time-consistency (jump protection) + recreated GIF
  Cars move smoothly but per-frame mono depth jitters, so the raw BEV "jumps". Added
  a constant-velocity KALMAN filter per car track (kalman_cv), filtering in the GLOBAL
  frame (where motion is physically smooth) with INNOVATION GATING: a measurement whose
  Mahalanobis distance exceeds chi2(2,99%)=9.21 is treated as a false/jittery detection
  and REJECTED (predict-only) -> the track cannot jump. Smoothed global positions are
  mapped back to the ego frame (global_to_cam) for display.
  TIME-CONSISTENCY METRIC (scene-0061, 10 tracks >=3 frames):
    track smoothness = RMS of per-frame 2nd-difference (accel): raw 6.99 m -> 2.19 m
        => 69% less jitter.
    gated jump-measurements: 5 ; implausible jumps (>12 m/frame): raw 4 -> 1.
    accuracy vs GT (global BEV): raw 4.93 m -> 4.66 m (preserved, slightly better).
  GIF recreated (viz_bev/bev_seq_scene-0061.gif, 39 frames): GT (teal o), raw pred
  (faint x), smoothed (red diamond) -> visibly steady vs the jittery raw markers.
  Tunables in kalman_cv: sigma_a (process accel std, smoothness), r_std (meas noise),
  gate (jump rejection threshold).

ROLLING-VIDEO METRIC (analogue of depth's d1; bev_tracker.py --consistency N)
  Depth d1 = per-pixel ratio threshold, static/per-frame -> blind to jitter on a video.
  Rolling-video score has TWO axes:
   (1) SPATIAL  BEV-delta@tau = fraction of car-obs within tau m of GT in BEV (the d1
       analogue; DISTANCE threshold not ratio; tau=1/2/4 m = nuScenes centre-distance).
   (2) TEMPORAL jitter = RMS per-frame step-acceleration of each track; + jump count.
  Over 6 nuScenes videos (867 car-obs, <=40 m), UniDepthV2-L:
    BEV-delta@{1,2,4}m:  raw 0.411/0.637/0.768   smoothed 0.332/0.579/0.762
    jitter (RMS accel):  raw 4.19 m -> smoothed 1.80 m  (57% less)
    jumps(>12m):         raw 25 -> smoothed 9   (gated 30 measurements)
    mean BEV err:        raw 3.39 m -> smoothed 4.04 m
  FINDING: Kalman cuts jitter 57% / jumps 3x but costs a little spatial accuracy
  (delta@1m 0.41->0.33) -> consistency<->accuracy trade-off, operating point set by the
  filter's process/measurement noise. Report BOTH axes (that's the point of the metric).
  Figure: viz_bev/bev_metric_table.png (src/make_bev_metric_table.py).

CORRECTION — d1 DOES transfer to video (per-frame + probabilistic)
  Earlier claim ("d1 is blind to jitter on a video") was too strong. Keeping d1
  PER-FRAME (not collapsing to a mean) and summarizing it as a DISTRIBUTION captures
  temporal consistency with the SAME metric: the std / low percentiles ARE the
  consistency signal. src/calib_depth/seq_d1.py.
  Per-frame depth d1 (UniDepthV2-L, nuScenes, <=80 m), 4 videos / 161 frames:
    pooled d1 mean 0.945  std 0.032  min 0.809  p10 0.905  p50 0.951  p90 0.976
    scene-0061 0.908+/-0.036 (dips to ~0.81 mid-clip = inconsistency) ;
    scene-0103 0.961+/-0.012 ; scene-0553 0.963+/-0.014 ; scene-0655 0.946+/-0.024.
  => a jumpy clip shows lower mean AND wider spread / lower min, same d1 metric.
  Figure: viz_bev/seq_d1.png (per-frame time series + distribution + mean/sigma).
  (BEV-delta@tau from the previous note is the spatial analogue for the *tracking*
  output; the same per-frame+distribution idea applies to it too.)
